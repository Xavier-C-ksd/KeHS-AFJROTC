Afterschool Activities And Teams

Armed Drill

Led by Commander Corbin Han, the Armed Drill Team performs advanced routines with rifles for competitions and assemblies. Participants must first complete Unarmed Drill. Armed Drill executes precise maneuvers, tosses, and spins, showcasing discipline and coordination.

Color Guard

Led by Commander Makayla Deen, Color Guard presents and posts the colors for ceremonies, including funerals, Veteran’s Day, and national events. They provide a positive representation of the Air Force and the unit, often performing at games, meetings, and competitions.

Unarmed Drill

Led by Commander Derek McMillen, Unarmed Drill Team focuses on marching and close-order drill without rifles. This team competes in events and exhibitions, supporting community events and school assemblies alongside the Color Guard.

Physical Training (P.T.)

Led by Commander Hunter Rigby, P.T. practices daily except Fridays, developing strength and endurance for competitions. Activities include running 3 miles, weightlifting, and bodyweight exercises in the classroom and gym.

Marksmanship

Led by Commander Sam Englert, this program teaches safety, discipline, and precision shooting at 10-meter ranges. Students compete in local competitions, including the Cascade Mountain Drill League, free for all enrolled cadets.

Kitty Hawk (Community Service)

Cadets participate in community service hours, including local events and service projects. Color Guard members often lead these activities, with adult supervision ensuring proper documentation and credit for service hours.

APT (Awareness Presentation Team)

Led by Jacob Slavens, the team promotes JROTC values in the community through middle school presentations and scheduled events, raising awareness and encouraging engagement with the corps.

Cyber Patriots

Led by Commander Luis Camacho, this team focuses on cybersecurity skills and competitions, meeting every Tuesday and Thursday from 3:30–5:00 PM to challenge students and build technical expertise.

StellarXplorers

A STEM-based space and aeronautics program open to all cadets. Students work on real-world problems and engineering tasks using tools like the STK simulator, developing skills in math, science, and aerospace disciplines.
